Tim Tebow: Quarterback's shirtless ad pulled as he refuses to strip down for Jockey underwear ads

He's fearless in the face of the NFL’s toughest linemen, but evidently Tim Tebow is a little shy when it comes to dropping trou for the camera.

The Denver Broncos quarterback and Jockey spokesman has refused to pose in only his underwear in ads for the brand, a move that's sure to disappoint his female fans.

Jockey, which has seen a jump in sales since Tebow joined their team last year, is surprisingly fine with it.

Tebow time: This Jockey ad, which features Tim Tebow's muscular physique accentuated by well-placed shadows, has been pulled from the site

Keeping the clothes on: The photo of a shirtless Tim Tebow on Jockey's website has since been replaced by three images of the quarterback fully clothed

Mo Moorman, the brand’s PR director, told The Huffington Post: 'Some things are better when left to the imagination'.

Jockey sales soared after a shirtless Tebow, his muscular features accentuated by well-placed shadows, graced their website last month.

That photo has since been replaced by three images of the quarterback: One in a t-shirt, one in a pullover and - gasp - one in a winter coat.

Clothed:  Tebow appears in more football-appropriate attire in new Jockey ads

Others disagree with Tebow’s insistence not to drop his drawers for Jockey, saying he's obligated to do so as the spokesman for a company that specialises in skivvies.

Marketing expert Stephen Bender told The Huffington Post: 'If you're not going to show the product, then how is the campaign going to work? You are either selling underwear or you're not.

Another sports star, David Beckham, has no problem for baring nearly all for the camera, launching yet another onslaught of underwear ads for his bodywear line for H&M.

Not so shy: David Beckham has stripped off for numerous underwear ads, including Armani and H&M

Couple: Beckham posed with his wife Victoria in this iconic Armani underwear ad

He has previously steamed up store windows in ads for Armani, including one well-known shot where he and his wife Victoria showcase their flesh in seductive poses.

Tebow's miracle season ended in a massacre on January 14 when the Broncos were trounced by Tom Brady and the New England Patriots.

In the regular season, Tebow became a gridiron hero with more late-game comebacks in his first 11 starts than any other NFL quarterback.

Ads: While Jockey believes Tebow's face can sell underwear, some say he's obligated to pose in his skivvies as an underwear company spokesman

These sporting heroics, as well as his devout religious beliefs have made Tebow something of a pop culture phenomenon.

He has single-handedly inspired the craze known as Tebowing - where fans mimic the quarterback's pre-game prayer stance in photos posted to Facebook and Twitter.

Recently, a driver was spotted who took very direct inspiration from Tebow, adorning his car with the licence plate 'WWTD15' - or 'What would Tebow do?', followed by his jersey number.

Tebow, who was a Heisman Trophy winner at the University of Florida, has also stirred controversy with his devout religious faith, and thanking his 'lord and saviour Jesus Christ' after victories.

Tebow, Tebowing: The unusual position the quarterback takes while praying on the field has inspired an internet craze, with people competing to pray in the most humorous situation

Celebrity status: Tebow is not only a favourite with Denver Broncos fans, his good looks and charm have seen him signed to powerful Hollywood agent Ari Emanuel
