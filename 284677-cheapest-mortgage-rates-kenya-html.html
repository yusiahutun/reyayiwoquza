Cheapest mortgage rates in Kenya

For many Kenyans, owning a home is a lifelong dream that is possible presently more than ever thanks to several cheap mortgage rates in Kenya today. A mortgage is a loan in which the property or real estate is used as collateral. The borrower (you) enters into an agreement with the lender (often a bank) to receive a lump sum that you can invest in your real estate venture and then pay back the loan and accrued interests over a given period of time. It is crucial to carry out in-depth research on several banks and their interest rates before applying for a mortgage.

There are a few key factors that you should consider when approaching a bank for any form of financing. The most important factor is to read their terms and conditions while paying particular attention to the costs percentage the loan will cover, loan repayment period and the interest rates. Looking for a mortgage is no different either. Before applying for a mortgage loan, you should take into consideration key factors such as interest rates, monthly payments, location and property price. It is also recommendable to use a bank that you love and trust.

Simple tips for securing the best mortgage rates in Kenya

There are several simple tips and tricks to ensure that you obtain the best mortgage rates in Kenya. When starting your search for the best mortgage provider, it is crucial to start off with thorough research on all possible options different banks offer. The truth is, numerous mortgage providers are willing to help you become a homeowner, but not all providers have affordable rates. It is, therefore, the borrower's duty to research on current mortgage interest rates in Kenya.

Here are some simple tips for securing cheap mortgage rates in Kenya.

1. Property price

According to real estate agents based in Nairobi, property prices in several neighborhoods in the city have doubled, and others even tripled, since 2006. For this reason, home buyers and future property owners are advised to carry out thorough research on property prices. One can employ the use of a housing finance consultant who can help you determine the most affordable property prices according to your income.

The same situation is repeated in Mombasa. Both residential and commercial buildings are getting expensively being sold even before construction is completed. That is why property price should be known first.

2. One huge deposit or monthly deposits

The age-old debate has always been whether it is better to reduce monthly mortgage repayments or to earn returns from another form of investment.

When it comes to the deposit, money bank mortgage rates in Kenya operate on a simple principle. The higher the deposit, the lower the loan you will need to borrow. The lower the amount you need to hire, the more the interest rates available to you. According to several housing finance consultants, it is always recommended to put down a deposit of at least 20% of the full payment.

3. The interest rates

Interest rates in Kenya are set according to the amount to be borrowed and the duration of the loan. At this stage, you should take the time to ask as many questions as possible concerning interest rates and the options available to them.

4. Monthly repayments

At this stage, you must consult with a housing finance provider or confirm on Kenyan real estate websites with mortgage calculators. The monthly repayment are easily calculated using the interest rate, property price, and deposit. These mortgage calculators on real estate websites provide a fast and straightforward way of determining monthly payments.

5. Location of the property

Often, in Kenya, houses in big cities are more likely to be covered by most commercial banks as compared to remote/ rural parts of the country. This is a trend often only seen with Kenyan banks. Very few banks agree to provide mortgage loans for rural homes.

Finally, you must note that mortgage rates in Kenya today fall under two categories; fixed rate mortgages and adjustable rate mortgages. Fixed rate mortgages allow the borrower to owe a percentage of the loan as interest. On the other hand, adjustable mortgage rates in Kenya reflect changes in the credit market in the repayment rates.
