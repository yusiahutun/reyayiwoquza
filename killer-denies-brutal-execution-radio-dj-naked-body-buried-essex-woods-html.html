Killer denies 'brutal execution' of radio DJ whose naked body was found buried in Essex woods'

An alleged killer has denied the 'brutal execution' of a radio DJ found buried in Essex woodland last year.

Ali Kavak, 25, denied murdering Koray Alpergin, 43, whose burned and naked corpse was discovered near the Oakwood Hill Industrial Estate in Loughton, Essex on 15 October.

Mr Alpergin was the owner of Turkish radio station Bizim FM, and had connections with celebrities such as rapper P Diddy and celebrity restauranteur Nusret Gökçe, otherwise known as 'Salt Bae'.

The alleged killer appeared at the Old Bailey today via video link along with Steffan Gordon, 33, who was charged with the murder in December and will enter pleas at a later date.

Koray Alpergin's (pictured) 'burned and bruised' body was found in a wooded area near the Oakwood Hill Industrial Estate in Loughton, Essex on October 15, 2022

Members of Mr Alpergin's family sobbed as Kavak denied the offences, which include kidnap and false imprisonment of Mr Alpergin and his friend Gozde Daibudak.

Junior Kettle, 31, did not appear today but is also charged with the murder.

Erdogan Ulcay, 55, and another male aged 17, are charged with perverting the course of justice by assisting with the disposal of Mr Alpergin's body.

Prosecutor Peter Ratcliffe said: 'This case involved the imprisonment, brutal torture and execution of Koray Alpergin.'

Prosecutor Nicholas Hearn earlier said: 'On 13 October, Mr Alpergin, the deceased, and his friend Gozde Dalbudak were returning to Mr Alpergin's home address in Enfield.

'They were attacked by individuals wearing white masks and bundled into a van and taken to a location and held there against their will.

Police believe that Mr Alpergin, who was the owner of London radio station Bizim FM, was forced into a white van with a 33-year-old woman outside their house in Enfield

Mr Alpergin was found near Oakwood Hill Industrial Estate in Loughton, Essex at 11.55am on Saturday, October 15, 2022 and was pronounced dead at the scene. Pictured: police vehicles at the scene on Oakwood Hill

'Mr Alpergin was badly beaten and murdered. On Saturday 15 October, two days later, Mr Alpergin's body was found by members of the public in a woodland area in Loughton, Essex.

'The body was naked and had sustained burns, bruises and cuts. A preliminary report concluded strangulation, blunt forced trauma and damage to the internal organs and ribs.'

A six week trial date is set for 18 September 2023.

A GoFundMe page, launched by Mr Alpergin's family and friends, reads: 'Koray was loved by everyone who had the pleasure of knowing him. A much-loved member of his family, a special friend to all and a huge member of the community with the biggest heart.

Prosecutor Nicholas Hearn told the court previously that Mr Alpergin and his friend Gozde Dalbudak were on their way back to the former's home in Enfield when the alleged incident took place

Junior Kettle, 31, is the fourth person to be charged with the murder of Koray Alpergin, 43

'Koray always thought of everyone, he was kind and caring and always supported those around him. He was such a light in the community, loved by his family, treasured by his friends and he lived his life to the fullest.'

Kavak, of De Quincey Road, Tottenham, denies the kidnap and false imprisonment of Mr Alpergin and Ms Daibudak and the murder of Mr Alpergin. He is also charged with perverting the course of justice and will enter a plea at a later date.

Kettle, of Kingsdown Road, Islington is charged with the murder of Mr Alpergin and the kidnap and false imprisonment of Mr Alpergin and Ms Daibudak.

Gordon, of Dehavilland Close, Northolt, is charged with the murder of Mr Alpergin.

Ulcay of Oakeshott Avenue, Camden and a 17-year-old are charged with perverting the course of justice.

They will next appear at court on 8 March.
