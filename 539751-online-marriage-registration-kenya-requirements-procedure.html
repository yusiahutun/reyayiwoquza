Online marriage registration in Kenya: Requirements and procedure

Thanks to the online application process, you no longer have to queue to register your marriage. The service is among over 5,000 government services available online through the e-citizen platform. So, what should you do to complete an online marriage registration in Kenya?

TABLE OF CONTENTS

Accessing marriage registration services online provides unparalleled convenience and flexibility while saving you time and money. It is now possible through the office of the Attorney General Registrar of Marriages. So, how do you go about it?

About the Registrar of Marriages

The Registrar of Marriages was established under Section 50 of the Marriage Act, and its mandate is to:

  • Perform civil marriages.
  • Register all marriages.
  • Issue marriage certificates for all registered marriages.
  • Issue certificates of no impediment to persons who intend to marry and qualify for such a certificate.
  • Determine the rules governing customary marriages.
  • Determine objections to notices to marry.

The Act recognises five marriage systems in Kenya, including civil, Hindu, customary, Christian, and Islamic marriages.

  • The services offered by the Registrar of Marriages include:
  • Registration of marriage by notice.
  • Registration of marriage by special license.
  • Application for a certified copy of a marriage certificate.
  • Application for a certificate of no impediment to marriage.
  • Registration of new and existing marriages.
  • Registration of foreign marriages.
  • Objections to marriage.
  • Licensing of ministers of faith.
  • Issuance of marriage books.

According to the Marriage Act, you can get married in Kenya if you are of legal age (18 and over) and comprehend marriage. The Act also defines marriage as a voluntary union between a man and woman, whether in a monogamous or polygamous union and registered under the Act.

Online marriage registration in Kenya

According to the official website of the Office of the Attorney General, online marriage services are only available in Nairobi County.

People in other locations and Nairobi can register their marriages at their local district/sub-county commissioner’s office. They can also register their marriages at their local Registrar of Marriages. So, how do you register your marriage on e-citizen?

Requirements for Christian and Hindu marriages

Before you fill out the online application to register your Christian or Hindu marriage, you are required to have the following documents on hand:

  • A copy of your national identity card, alien card or valid passport.
  • A copy of your birth certificate.
  • A coloured passport-size photo of each applicant.
  • A copy of the death certificate (if a widow or widower).
  • Copy of divorce decree absolute (if the partner has been married before and divorced)
  • An affidavit (sworn) to confirm the marital status in case the divorce decree or death certificate is over two years old.
  • Copy of license for the minister of faith.
  • Serial number of the marriage book to be used.
  • Wedding invitation card.

All applicants should pay a notice fee of Ksh.600 on the online platform. Once the 21 days have elapsed (Notice period), the parties involved must book an appointment via the online portal to appear before the Registrar of Marriages for an interview and verification of documents.

Once the applicants are successful, they must pay Ksh.800 for the clearance certificate online. They are required to part with Ksh.7,200 if the wedding occurs at a licenced place of worship.

Requirements for the registration of customary marriages

The requirements for the notice of registration of customary marriages include:

  • Copy of national identity card or passport.
  • Copies of national identity cards of witnesses who were present at the time of the ceremony.
  • Coloured passport-size photo.
  • A letter from the chief confirming that the customary marriage took place.

Parties must place a notice within three months after completing the necessary steps to confer husband and wife status. On top of this, the registration must happen within six months from the date of marriage.

It is important to note that the parties who intend to enter a customary marriage must not have entered a civil or Christian marriage at the time of registration.

After fourteen days, both parties must book an appointment online and appear before the Registrar of Marriages for submission of original documents and interview. Upon approval, the applicants will pay a Ksh.3,900/= online.

Requirements for civil marriage

The following are marriage certificate requirements on e-citizen for a civil marriage:

  • Copy of national identity card, valid passport or alien card.
  • Copy of birth certificate.
  • One coloured passport-sized photograph.
  • Copy of death certificate (widower or widow).
  • Affidavit if the divorce decree or death certificate is over two years old.
  • Copies of identity cards or valid passports of two witnesses.

People applying for civil marriages are required to pay Ksh.600 online upon approval. Once 21 days have gone, they must book an appointment to verify the original documents. After approval, they can pay Ksh.3,300 and be secured to solemnise their marriage.

How to apply marriage certificate online in Kenya?

Applying online is relatively straightforward:

  • Head to the e-citizen portal for registration of marriages: oag.ecitizen.go.ke
  • Log in using your credentials.
  • Choose the type of marriage you want to register and click next.
  • Read the instructions, make sure you have all the documents required, and click next.
  • Choose the county, venue of marriage, proposed date, and serial number of the marriage book and click next.
  • Enter the ensuing details, including applicant, bride, and bride-groom details, and process all uploads.
  • Once done, wait for the days mentioned in the instructions for your notice to mature.

Is online marriage allowed in Kenya?

Online marriage services are available in Kenya, and one can visit the e-citizen portal, Registrar of Marriages, to start their registration. However, it is essential to note that the service is available for Nairobi County only.

People taking part in a customary marriage should pay Ksh.3,900 on the online portal upon approval. On the other hand, participants in a civil marriage pay Ksh.600 for the notice and Ksh.3,300 for the solemnisation of their marriage.
