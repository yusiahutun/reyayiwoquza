Bare knuckle fighter was &quot;p***ing blood&quot; to make weight before quitting boxing

A bare knuckle fighter has opened up about the effect of his extreme weight loss tactics during his former career as a boxer, and argued that his new sport is actually safer inside and outside the ring.

Tyler Goodjohn began his professional sporting career as a boxer, even supporting Anthony Joshua before having his licence removed for revealing the sport’s lack of support for injured fighters.

The Cambridge-born scrapper quickly switched sports, and has since gone on to forge a successful career in bare knuckle fighting. And, despite the sport’s reputation for violence and horrific facial injuries, Tyler has claimed that he feels safer without gloves.

Interviewing Tyler on his Anything Goes podcast, James English asked Tyler how he had dealt with meeting his weight categories while he was boxing.

“When I went up to bare knuckle I moved up to 11 stone, so when I was a professional glove boxer I was fighting at ten stone, so I was having to do stupid things to make that weight,” he remembered.

“The last time I made ten stone I lost 18 pounds in the last week. I was p***ing blood and all sorts, I was so dehydrated.

“But when the fights are coming at that weight – the money fights –you just keep trying to get down to that weight.”

Since his career switch Tyler now weighs in around the 13 stone mark, and he admitted that he finds it much easier to keep his diet on track in his new sport.

“You’re not doing all those stupid things in the final week. You’ll train for ten or 11 weeks, for a fight, train your arse off and then in that last week you’ll ruin everything by dehydrating so badly that you can’t even think straight,” he continued.

In fact, Tyler Tyler recalled feeling “two seconds behind” during the warm-up for his final bout because of his weight loss exertions.

“I went out there and I just got pummelled, I was like a punchbag basically, and after that fight I was like, ‘I’m done with this. I’ve got to move up otherwise I’ll kill myself’,” he added.

And, despite the violent, and often bloody, nature of bare knuckle scraps, Tyler claimed that he also felt safer inside the ring than when he had been sparring with gloves.

“With bare knuckle, facially obviously the injuries are a lot worse, but I actually think it’s safer than boxing because you’re just not taking the shots that you would for a prolonged amount of time,” he explained.

“People who have sort of had bleeds on the brain and things like that have been from ten or 12 round fights where they’ve taken a lot of punishment.

“Whereas bare knuckle is five two-minute rounds, and let’s face it, if you get caught clean, you’re getting knocked out.

“So I argue with people all the time; bare knuckle is safer than gloved boxing.”

On the same podcast episode, Tyler also opened up about the personal issues he had battled while striving to perform as a professional athlete, admitting that a ‘sex addiciton’ had resulted in him making a brief stint as a porn star.
