Hot TV: Jimmy Bullard's set to make a grilling

Fans tune in for hit series including A Question Of Sport and Soccer AM every week.

But jungle star Jimmy said: “Even top presenters go around the edges. They sometimes don’t ask that crunch question. That’s what is missing from TV.”

Jimmy, 37, is fronting his own show on London Live which sees him grill celeb guests and give frank opinions of the latest footie matches.

He said: “I want this show to be about the truth. I forget the cameras and you get the real Jimmy Bullard – what I really feel. I let you have it as it really is.”

Jimmy, who divided opinion on I’m A Celebrity, added: “It doesn’t matter if I upset people. You just move on and deal with it.

“A little bit of hard telling isn’t going to hurt anyone. Let’s just hope we don’t go over the top.”

Jimmy Bullard Kicks Off airs every Monday and Friday at 7pm on London Live (Freeview 8, YouView 8, Sky 117 and Virgin 159).

SHOW hunk Michael Fox has revealed he is constantly bombarded with questions from his loved ones about what it’s like to work on Downton.

The actor, who plays footman Andy Parker in the popular period drama, said: “Anyone I talk to, I’m met with so much excitement. My mum and nan are very excited by it.

“My mum even came to the set and had a wander around – she just loves the show so much.”

He added: “Everyone on it is very humble. Each new series, we’ve got something to prove.

"We arrived to do this series with a feeling we needed to do the same again after five years of great work.”

Downton continues tonight at 9pm on ITV

GAIL Emms says retired sports stars are at risk of going off the rails because there is no help available.

The Olympic badminton star, who has struggled with depression since quitting, said: “I don’t think there is enough support as it is such a unique experience.

“I am currently studying in coaching and mentoring and want to help athletes in the transition from sport to everyday life.”

Fans have seen Gail, 38, compete against other retired stars on Eternal Glory.

Speaking at the new Premier Inn in Hitchin, she added: “I’d love to do the Bear Grylls show – I love anything with a challenge.”
