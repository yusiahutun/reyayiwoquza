Trendy Queen Flannel Shacket

When you think of a shacket, a plaid button-down like this Trendy Queen style is probably the first image that comes to mind. The overshirt comes in 11 color combinations and is made from a “soft and good-quality flannel” material, per a reviewer. Wear it casually with leggings, a basic tee, and Ugg boots, or dress it up with dark denim, a tight-fitting tank, and heeled booties. 

Hotouch Waffle-Knit Shacket

If you prefer a solid-colored option, this waffle-knit shacket comes in 16 shades with contrasting buttons. The perfectly oversized style is “thick, well-made, and comfy,” per a reviewer, and made from a machine-washable blend of polyester and spandex. Throw it on over a T-shirt and sweats to lounge around during the day, and spice it up with faux leather leggings and a body-hugging undershirt for a night out. 

Dokotoo Oversized Distressed Denim Shacket

Of course, we had to include a denim option, too, and this Dokotoo style is at the top of our list. It has a frayed hemline for a lived-in feel, along with statement silver buttons down the front and two envelope-style chest pockets up top. Go for a simple look with black leggings and a tee underneath, or channel your inner Britney and Justin with a denim-on-denim Canadian tuxedo look. 

Merokeety Oversized Wool-Like Shacket

Another laid-back style, this Merokeety wool-like shacket has slouchy drop shoulders, an oversized fit, and convenient side pockets. Plus, the snaps down the front make the overshirt super easy to open and close during the day as the weather changes. Not to mention, a shopper was “absolutely shocked at the level of quality” in the construction of the shacket, adding that it “looks like a very expensive piece.” 

Dokotoo Corduroy Shacket

A corduroy piece is a must in your fall wardrobe, and this boxy shacket is a great way to try out the trend. Available in 12 colors, the shacket has silver buttons down the front, two chest pockets, and an unfinished hemline. It’ll look great over a T-shirt during these beginning weeks of fall, and then you can wear it “over a crewneck for a layered look” as it gets colder, according to a reviewer.
