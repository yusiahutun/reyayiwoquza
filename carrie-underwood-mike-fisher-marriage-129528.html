Carrie Underwood and Her Husband Mike Fisher's Marriage May Be in Trouble

Say it ain’t so!

Carrie Underwood and her husband, Mike Fisher, could be headed for a split after nearly seven years of wedded bliss.

According to a recent report, the 34-year-old American Idol winner and her pro hockey player husband’s marriage may be on the rocks because they’re “leading separate lives.”

“Between their careers, Carrie and Mike are so busy that they’re barely together — and unfortunately, their problems run much deeper than that,” a source revealed to Star.

“I believe Carrie feels Mike doesn’t see their relationship as a priority, and that he’s not putting forth the effort to make her happy and feel secure,” the insider continued. “They’re in a very vulnerable place, and their friends are worried they’ve drifted so far apart that they may not be able to find their way back together again.”

“Unfortunately, they just can’t seem to connect, and Carrie’s devastated,” the source said, adding the country star feels “lonely and abandoned” in her union. “Carrie’s friends are encouraging her to explore her options, but her mom Carole, is urging her to try to fix her marriage.”

Though the “Smoke Break” singer could be unhappy in her relationship with Mike, 37, the insider claimed Carrie won’t ever leave her athlete hubby. “Carrie’s a devout Christian who takes her vows very seriously, and I would have to think any suspicions about Mike would eat her up inside. But, leaving him is almost unimaginable because of her beliefs in the sanctity of marriage,” the source added.
