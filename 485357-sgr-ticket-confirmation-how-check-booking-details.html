SGR ticket confirmation: How to check your booking details

Since its introduction in June 2017, the Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) train has been serving a large number of people daily. However, the company has a strict ticketing policy and you will not be refunded your money if the travel date has already passed. SGR ticket confirmation is the only tool that you have at your disposal to check your booking details.

SGR is a standard-gauge railway in Kenya that connects the city of Mombasa with Nairobi. There are two types of trains. The Madaraka Express train does not make a stop during the journey. On the other hand, the Inter-county passenger train makes a stop at respective stations namely Athi River, Emali, Kibwezi, Mtito Andei, Miasenyi and Mariakani.

How to book your SGR ticket

When booking an SGR ticket, you have two options. You can be physically present at one of the stations to purchase it. The tickets are available at the counters. Alternatively, you can make your life simple by booking your ticket online from the convenience of your home or office. Here is a simple procedure you should follow to book your ticket online.

  • Open the KRC e-ticket portal on your device.
  • Search for either Express or Inter-County Train and fill in your travel details.
  • You should select your origin and destination, travel date, train, preferred class, seat(s), and provide personal information.
  • Pay for your ticket via M-Pesa.
  • After successfully making the payment, you will receive an SMS or e-mail confirmation with a unique code.

How to get your ticket after booking SGR online

You can print your train ticket at the station at the self-service machines found at the ticketing offices at all stations prior to boarding the train. Once you have paid for your SGR ticket via M-PESA, you will receive a confirmation SMS on the number you used to make payment.

You should not delete the message because you will need the e-ticket confirmation code to print your ticket at the terminus. The e-ticket confirmation code is a string of seven (7) letters that can be found on the message received upon booking.

In case you experience any challenges when printing the tickets, you should seek assistance from the Passenger Stewards at the ticketing office. The tickets can also be printed over the counter.

SGR ticket confirmation

Before the travel date, you need to confirm your SGR ticket to know the travel date, departure time, coach number, and seat number. If you are stuck on how to confirm your SGR ticket, you should not panic. You can present the e-ticket confirmation code at the counter of the SGR station to confirm the booking details.

If you cannot be physically present at an SGR office to confirm your booking details, you can contact the customer care team to confirm the booking details. Unfortunately, there is no set procedure on how to check your SGR ticket through SMS.

SGR ticket delays

Sometimes the system may fail to send the ticket purchase confirmation message. If you experience this problem, you should not panic. The M-Pesa transaction message you received after making payment can be used to print the ticket at the train station before departure.

All you need to do is key in the mobile number you used to transact. You will also be required to provide the account number the funds were sent to. The account number is contained in the M-pesa message.

If you have any questions about SGR ticket, you can present them at the counter of any SGR station. Alternatively, you can contact the customer care team using the following details:

  • SGR customer care number: 0709 388 887/ 0709 388 888/ 0709 907 000
  • Email: contact@krc.co.ke
  • Postal address: P.O Box 3012-Nairobi, 00100, Kenya

There you have it. A comprehensive guide on SGR ticket confirmation. After booking your ticket online, it is important that you confirm the travel date, departure time, coach number, and seat number. If you fail to check your booking details, there is a high chance that you will lose your hard earned money because there are no refunds for tickets if the travel time is already past.
