Compare Costs, Reviews, and More

When you’ve shopped for hair care in the past, it was hard to ignore the bright labels promising volume or bold fonts highlighting anti-frizz benefits. But how many times did those shampoo and conditioners, regardless of the brand’s popularity, fall flat? While “customized” has become a favored buzzword in the beauty industry, particularly when it comes to hair care products, there’s still some validation to the idea. Because, let’s be frank, hair is very personal; there’s not—or at least shouldn’t be—a one-size-fits-all solution for the general population. and our precious locks deserve some attention.

When it comes to customized hair care, two companies that have propelled the movement forward are Prose and Function of Beauty. There are quite a few similarities between both companies, such as subscription options, natural ingredients, and product range, but they both have several key differences, too. Here, we compare Prose and Function of Beauty so that you can better decide which, if either, are a good fit for you.

What Is Prose?

Established in France in 2017, but based out of Brooklyn, Prose specializes in customized hair care products. Like other similar services, the experience begins with a “hair quiz” that asks typical questions about hair texture, oil and dryness level, hair loss, and more. But unlike many companies, Prose’s questionnaire dives a little deeper, covering topics like hair styling and treatments, diet, stress levels, and zip code, the last of which immediately generates information about your area’s environment (pollution, UV rays, water hardness, humidity, wind). Plus, each question, of which there are about 30, is accompanied by either a reason for why it’s being asked or a correlated fact, oftentimes explaining how something can affect your hair.

Following your results, you’ll learn more about the ingredients that will be used in your customized product. Prose prides itself on utilizing natural ingredients devoid of parabens, sulfates, GMOs, mineral oils, and animal cruelty; supplements are also gluten-free. You’ll be provided with a list of product recommendations based on your hair and scalp needs, and then can purchase the items one time or on a subscription basis. Subscribers will receive Prose products on a monthly, bimonthly, or quarterly basis, and also receive a 15 percent discount and a free product for every 10 products purchased. The best part? Prose has a 100 percent satisfaction guarantee, so if you’re not happy with your results, you can request a full refund or request for an adjusted formula free of charge within 30 days of delivery.

What Is Function of Beauty?

Founded in 2015 by two MIT engineers and a chemist, Function of Beauty is often credited for being the company to popularize customizable hair care products. Its quick and easy quiz sticks to the basic questions, like hair type, scalp moisture, and hair goals. Once the results are processed, you’ll have the chance to learn more about the vegan and cruelty-free ingredients that’ll be used in your products. In addition to selecting your desired fragrance and fragrance strength, you’ll be able to personalize the bottle and choose the color for your shampoo and conditioner, adding an extra layer of customization.

When checking out, you’ll find a list of suggested products based on your quiz results and hair goals. While there’s an option to make a one-time purchase, subscribers benefit from perks that include free shipping and members-only discounts. Other hair care products, unfortunately, are not eligible for a discount on automatic monthly, bimonthly, or quarterly deliveries. However, like Prose, Function of Beauty offers full refunds within 30 days of receipt if you’re not happy with your order. And while the company is best known for hair care products, it has also expanded into body wash, lotion, and skincare since its inception, making it a one-stop shop for customizable beauty products.
