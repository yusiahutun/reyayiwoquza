Andrea Casiraghi and Tatiana Santo Domingo take baby out

Almost a year and a half after their first child was born, Andrea Casiraghi and Tatiana Santo Domingo thrilled locals when they took their baby son Sacha out for his first engagement. The trio were attending Monaco's National Day on Wednesday, with other members of the Monegasque royal family in attendance. Unaware of the stir he was causing, baby Sacha looked the picture of cuteness as he cuddled up to his mum Tatiana. The flaxen-haired boy was dressed in a white shirt and adorable dungarees, and kept warm in a beige coat.

Tatiana Santo Domingo welcomed her baby son Sacha last year

Tatiana held her toddler in her arms as the mother-son duo watched the parade from the royal balcony, with Tatiana's mother-in-law Princess Caroline at their side. Fashion designer Tatiana, who is pregnant with her second child, looked as chic as ever in a charcoal and black straight-cut patterned coat, which she topped off with an elegant black hat.

Proud dad Andrea Casiraghi held tightly on to his son Sacha

During the festivities, the royals had kicked off their celebrations in the palace's courtyard. Princess Caroline, who donned a navy, tweed jacket and skirt, kept a watchful eye over her baby grandson and was pictured holding his right hand, while proud dad Andrea took his left. Other members of Monaco's royal family made their appearances, including the pregnant Princess Charlene who is expected to give birth to twins at the end of the year.

Princess Charlene and Prince Albert share a rare public display of affection

Standing on the balcony, Charlene and her husband Prince Albert put on a rare public display of affection as they leant in and shared a tender kiss. The reigning monarch, 56, has previously revealed that he was "overjoyed" to hear that his wife was pregnant with their first children. Princess Caroline's sister Princess Stephanie was also pictured attending the National Day, as well as Caroline's younger children Pierre Casiraghi and Princess Alexandra.
