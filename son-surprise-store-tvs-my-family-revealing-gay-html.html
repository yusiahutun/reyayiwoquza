Son has a surprise in store for TV's My Family by revealing he is gay

Son Michael has a surprise in store for TV's My Family by revealing he is gay

It is known for gently poking fun at middle class angst as it follows the domestic dramas of a grumpy dentist and his wife and children.

But now BBC sitcom My Family is being shaken up, with producers revealing one of its longest-standing characters is gay.

Show sources claim that Michael, the youngest son of characters Ben and Susan Harper, will 'come out' to his parents, played by Robert Lindsay and Zoe Wanamaker, at the start of the tenth series.

Family fun: The cast of the long-running BBC comedy, featuring Robert Lindsay and Zoe Wannamaker on the sofa and Gabriel Thompson, who plays Michael second from right, celebrate their 100th episode

The news comes despite student Michael, played by Gabriel Thomson, having been transformed from a geeky schoolboy into something of a womaniser over the past few series.

A show insider said that a team of new writers came up with the twist after being brought in to make the sitcom more 'edgy and relevant'.

The source said: 'We were given a brief to turn the show into something a younger audience would relate to and watch.

'This is just one of the storylines we have come up with. It's handled very sensitively.'

A spokesman for gay rights organisation Stonewall said: 'It's refreshing that My Family will be reflecting modern Britain in their next series.'

Thomson, 23, has starred in My Family since its premiere in 2000.

Young one: Viewers have been following the fortunes of Gabriel Thomson since My Family first aired in 2000

He is the only one out of the actors who play the Harper children to have appeared in every series as Daniela Denby Ashe, who played daughter Janey left in 2002, before returning in 2004, and Kris Marshall, who played dysfunctional son Nick left the series in 2005. 

The young star, who has been acting since the age of five, has appeared with Jude Law in the film Enemy at the Gates, and played young Pip in a BBC production of Great Expectations – as well as being linked with the role of Harry Potter in the big-screen adaption, before the job went to Daniel Radcliffe.

However, he was arrested and cautioned by police for possession of class A drugs in 2008.

My Family follows the troubles and travails of grumpy dentist Ben and his wife Susan, a control freak and terrible cook, alongside their three children and equally odd hangers on.

It has been proved to be one the most enduring and successful British sitcoms of recent times and has survived as one of the last traditional sitcoms on our screens.

Overall, series nine was the most watched comedy series of 2009, regularly pulling in more than seven million viewers per episode. It was followed by a Christmas special that got six million.

Last September it was reported that BBC bosses had cut the show’s budget by 15 per cent and commissioned a tenth and eleventh series of the sitcom.

Corporation chiefs had initially ordered a 10 per cent pay cut for actors Robert Lindsay and Zoë Wannamaker to be ring-fenced within the reduction, but producer DLT Entertainment negotiated a less severe cut.

Don Taffner Jr, president of DLT and executive producer of My Family admitted at the time: ‘The BBC proposals put the show at risk for a while.’

To reduce costs, the two series will be made back to back and have already gone into production.

The BBC has still not placed the tenth series in the schedules, but last night a BBC spokesman said it would be shown later this year.
