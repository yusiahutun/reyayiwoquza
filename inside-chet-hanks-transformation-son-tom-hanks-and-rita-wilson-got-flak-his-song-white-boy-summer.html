Inside Chet Hanks transformation: the son of Tom Hanks and Rita Wilson got flak for his song Whit

At the end of July, Chet revealed to his more than 530,000 followers on Instagram that he had chosen a new path in life, stating, “There’s only one thing the world needs more of and that’s God.” In the post, Chet shared an image of his chest with a cross tattooed over his previous Eye of Providence tattoo, explaining that he was an atheist before converting to Christianity 10 years ago. According to Men’s Health, Chet deleted all other images from his Instagram account, leaving only the one of his chest.

“All the pain in my life has been the result of one thing – the lie of believing that my purpose is anything but just serving God,” he wrote. “It’s not fully clear to me yet how I can best serve Him, but I feel that this level of honesty is a starting point. And I know He will continue to reveal more.”

Chet later deleted the image of his chest and his caption, leaving his page completely blank.

He is an actor and a rapper

The first child of actress Rita Wilson and Tom Hanks, Chet, 33, was born on August 4, 1990. As a student at Northwestern University, he used the stage name Chet Haze while attempting to launch a music career.

He has also done some acting work and his well-known for his roles on Empire and Shameless. He famously made headlines when he confirmed via an Instagram video that his parents had Covid-19 at the start of the pandemic.

However, he raised eyebrows for his use of AAVE in the video, The New Yorker reported. He drew attention again in another Instagram video, where he stated, “I just got this feeling, man, that this summer is, it’s about to be a white boy summer.” He released his hip-hop song “White Boy Summer” in mid-2021.

His message drew sharp criticism for his apparent lack of understanding of the rise of white supremacist violence in the US, per The New Yorker. At the same time though, he has also blasted former US president Donald Trump and shown his support for the Black Lives Matter movement.

In 2022, he opened the personal training company HanxFit.

Struggles with substance abuse

In 2014, he was admitted to rehab to treat substance abuse – a problem he had battled since he was 16. He said he got sober for his daughter, Michaiah Hanks, who he had with his ex Tiffany Miles, per The New Yorker.

In a podcast with Ivan Paychecks, Chet spoke about the interventions his parents made at the height of his addiction. In 2008, when he was a high school junior, he was dragged out of his bed and taken to a wilderness programme in Utah. But Chet believes his parents were simply manipulated by and taken advantage of by the programme because they had money.

His Hollywood legacy was a “double-edged sword”

In a YouTube video, in February 2022, Chet opened up about having famous parents. He said growing up in Hollywood was a “double-edged sword” and while he is often seen as a spoiled brat, he wasn’t raised that way.

Chet acknowledged that he was privileged, but said he was “never just given money or an allowance”. He had to work to earn money and was raised that way his whole life, he added. Chet went on to declare that fame is the “most powerful drug known to man. It can also be the most destructive”.
