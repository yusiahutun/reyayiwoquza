Rihanna casually steps out in $400,000 watch - but she's not wearing it on her wrist

Rihanna has just stepped the fashion up to a place we didn’t think it could go as she was spotted wearing a $400,000 watch around her ankle.

The music star has been living it up in Las Vegas this weekend alongside her partner, A$AP Rocky as they attended the F1 Grand Prix. However, all eyes were on RiRi and her incredible fashion.

Rihanna wears $400,000 watch as an anklet

The singer may have started the next fashion trend with this one. Just months after wearing a Jacob & Co. watch around her neck in Paris, she’s now one-upped even herself with the anklet.

The piece worn around her ankle also comes from Jacob & Co, and is said to be embellished with over 70 carats of diamonds and set in 18K white gold.

The bling around her leg stood out with her chic all-black outfit. The Diamonds singer sported over a pair of figure-hugging black Balenciaga leggings, easy for the watch to be placed over.

Matching her leggings, she wore a Balenciaga black leather jacket, and an unzipped black hoodie, showing a sneak peek of her bra. Fenty, we’re guessing.

The 35-year-old wore her new blonde hairdo in effortless waves as she added a pop of color with a bright red lip. We’re guessing Fenty again.

New pics of Rihanna and ASAP have fans ‘crying’

It looks like the music stars are having a busy weekend. As well as their trip to Vegas, they’re sending X, formerly known as Twitter into a meltdown with adorable new pics.

Fans couldn’t get enough of how Rihanna is looking at A$AP, as they took to the social media platform to gush over the couple.

“Find someone that looks at you how Rihanna looks at ASAP,” wrote one.

“The things I will do to have her look at me like that!” another joked.

Another wrote: “I wanna be just like this,” honestly, us too.

The pair made a surprise appearance at the launch of the new Puma x F1 collection, Less than a month after A$AP Rocky was revealed as the creative director for PUMA’s new partnership with F1.

RiRi herself is the creative director of FENTY x PUMA, and together, they’ve just released the return of the iconic creeper.
