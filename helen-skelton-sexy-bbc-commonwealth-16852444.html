Helen Skelton 'kicked off BBC' due to revealing and racy outfits

Reports claims BBC regular Helen Skelton won't be heading down under with the rest of the Beeb's team, due to her overly sexual outfits.

During her time hosting the Rio Olympics on the BBC, the blonde beauty sent social media ablaze thanks to her stunning good looks and fondness of a plunging neckline.

Now, the BBC have stepped in with the hopes of making their coverage more conservative.

Sources close to Helen, 34, told Mail Online how she is "sad" about the decision.

However, while the BBC have confirmed she will not be travelling to Australia for the sporting event with them, they claim it is simply due to a "smaller production" than the 2014 Commonwealth Games in Glasgow.

A source said: "They [BBC] haven't explained their reasons.

"There are a few people where you think it's a bit weird they aren't there. Helen would have loved to be there."

A spokesman for BBC Sport said: "We are sending a considerably smaller operation to Australia than to previous games.

"Helen remains a key presenter."

Instead, Clare Balding, Gabby Logan, Jason Mohammad, Hazel Irvine will all lead the TV coverage.

Meanwhile, Invictus Games host JJ Chalmers has also been added to the hosting line-up.

Last year, Helen openly boasted about her wild sex life during an appearance on Lorraine.

After hearing Dr Hilary Jones say that men who spend hours pumping iron in the gym every day end up burning out before they can get down to any bedroom action, Helen declared: "Well, I'm eight months pregnant and my husband's in the gym every day."

Grinning, she added: "So draw your own conclusions."

Helen has been with her rugby playing hubby Richie Myler since 2012.

After tying the knot the following year, Helen gave birth to their first child in 2015 before they welcomed their second baby two years later.
