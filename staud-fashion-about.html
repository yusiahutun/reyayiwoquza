Everything to know about fashion label Staud Interview with Sarah Staudinger

Courtesy of Staud

Each week, we look into a new, exciting and innovative label that is taking the fashion world by storm in our regular feature #TheBrand. This time, we’re turning the spotlight on vintage-inspired Californian label Staud.

Known best for its cult handbags and retro ready-to-wear, the brand has fast become a firm favourite on the fashion week front rows. Here we break down everything you need to know about the label.

Origins

Founded in California in 2015 by Sarah Staudinger and her partner George Augusto, Staud began as an attempt to fill a gap in the market for “a high-end aesthetic at an accessible price point”. The label strives to create vintage-inspired pieces that you will actually be able to wear season after season, rather than purchasing something to tick off a particular trend.

“We wanted to create collections that were not overly trend driven that had a timeless quality and remain affordable,” Staudinger told us.

Quirky handbags, ’50s-inspired jumpsuits and frocks that look as if they were made for the LA sunshine certainly meet this aesthetic.

Why is everyone talking about it?

Although Staud has a strong presence in the ready-to-wear market, the brand’s accessories are where it has really made a mark, with a number of its bags becoming ‘It’ pieces of the season, particularly the Moreau bucket bag, which was carried by every influencer worth her salt during the shows in February.

“We think our bags are unique and fun but at the same time have a really timeless essence to them,” Staudinger said of the success. “I think our customers see their style versatility and are excited about creating outfits with them.”

The bags are certainly statement and instantly recognisable but come at a reasonably affordable price point when it comes to ‘It’ bags, all falling under the £500 mark.

Who has worn it?

Alexa Chung and Dakota Fanning are just two of the A-list faces to have shown support by wearing Staud – and plenty of influencers across the globe have been spotted in it.

“We try not to overthink who we would want wearing Staud, we like to see who gravitates naturally to the brand so nothing feels forced,” Staudinger tells us, but is quick to admit that the power of influencers has been fundamental to the label’s success.

“They have been hugely important. We’ve been so fortunate so many amazing women have reached out and support us or responded when we’ve reached out to them. We could not have had this trajectory without them.”

What has been key to their success?

A lot of what appeals about Staud’s designs is that LA aesthetic so many of us want to incorporate into our wardrobes. Staudinger says that this is all about attitude.

“I think we represent California not so much through aesthetic but through our attitude towards fashion. We don’t take it too seriously and we definitely try to instil as much playfulness as we can in our designs. Value is also a big component when we design. Not so much about creating the least expensive item, but making the girl who buys us feel like she’s getting something really beautiful and high quality at our price point.”

Having this value at the core of the business has been fundamental to Staud’s success, but not this alone.

“It really is a culmination of many things. So much hard work, staying authentic to what you love and not just what you think will sell, building a team that is relentless in achieving your vision, and staying humble.”

What’s next?

Being such a new company, Staud is concentrating on the success of the moment, rather than plotting for the future.

“We’re so new that we’re not looking too far ahead," Staudinger says. "Right now it’s about perfecting our product, making sure the fit and quality are the best it can be for our price point, and finding the right balance between ecommerce and wholesale.”

Key pieces

The influencer-approved Moreau bucket bag is a must, while Staud's summer dress collection is extremely covetable, particularly the nautical-inspired yellow linen dress.
