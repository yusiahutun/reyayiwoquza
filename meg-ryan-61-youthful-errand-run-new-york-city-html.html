Meg Ryan put on a youthful display sporting an all-black ensemble as she stepped out for an errand run in New York City on Friday.

The Sleepless In Seattle actress, 61 — who will make her film comeback in the upcoming rom-com What Happens Later — was seen strolling through the bustling city streets during her weekday outing.

The Golden Globe-nominated star wore a short-sleeved, plain black shirt along with a black skirt that contained a hem that fell down past her knees. 

She slipped into a pair of black sneakers that were secured with laces to stay comfortable as she made her way through the Big Apple. 

Her locks were parted in the middle, and flowed down naturally to easily brush her shoulders.

Meg accessorized her look by wearing a glittering, silver bracelet on her left wrist as well as a flashy, diamond ring on her right hand. 

During the outing, the When Harry Met Sally actress also showed off a 'Life Is Short' tattoo on her left arm which she had gotten years ago in Brooklyn. 

During a past interview with The Los Angeles Times in 2016, she had opened up about what the phrase meant to her and the importance of always looking forward. 

'You know what you know and you do what you do,' Ryan informed the outlet, adding, 'And hopefully you're just alive and curious.'

The mother of two was seen carrying a black tote bag filled with items from the health food restaurant, Just Plants by Juice Press. 

She also threw on a pair of silver-rimmed shades for a fashionable flare and to protect her eyes when the sun peaked through the layer of clouds. 

Meg - who was previously married to fellow actor Dennis Quaid - has been preparing her anticipated comeback to the big screen in the romantic comedy titled, What Happen Later. 

The film was co-written and directed by Ryan, who also tackled a leading role in the project alongside The X-Files alum, David Duchovny.

While recently talking to Carol Burnett for Interview Magazine, the You've Got Mail actress opened up about working on her latest project - which is slated to release in theaters on November 3. 

The premise follows, 'Willa (Ryan) and Bill (Duchovny) are ex-lovers that will see each other for the first time in years when they both find themselves snowed in, in-transit, at an airport overnight,' per an official IMDB synopsis. 

Burnett brought up the 'chemistry' between Meg and David in the rom-com, and Ryan admitted, 'Well, I didn't really know him before. We'd only met a couple of times, maybe 20 years ago.' 

She later added that, 'He's really smart and he's such a unique kind of funny that we all thought he would be so perfect for Bill.' 

What Happens Later is based on a play called, Shooting Star, which was written by playwright Steven Dietz and the star discussed working on set with Duchovny. 

'We shot over the course of 21 nights and we did maybe three or four takes. Neither of us wanted to be up past 9:30a.m.,' and added, 'We liked being there together and we had a fantastic crew that we really trusted. It was as fun as it looks...'

In regards to the plot and meaning of the film, Meg explained, 'The story started to revolve around this question of how we begin to forgive ourselves and one another.'

She continued, 'It's a rom-com, so a lot of times you have to ask those deeper questions in secret and hope that people can feel them underneath everything.'

What Happens Later will be Meg's first film in eight years - with her last project being Ithaca in 2015. 

Ryan previously opened up about her hiatus from the entertainment industry during an interview with Goop founder Gwyneth Paltrow back in 2018, per Entertainment Tonight. 'I didn't really aim to be an actor.' 

'I was a journalism major at school, and a curious person, and I wanted to go back out into the world and figure out who I was, am, in relation to other things and other people and other environments,' she explained. 

While talking to The New York Times in 2019, she further opened about taking a break and leaving L.A. for NYC. 'I was burned out. I didn't feel like I knew enough anymore about myself or the world to reflect it as an actor. I felt isolated.'
