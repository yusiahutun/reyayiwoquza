Source: Watch.plex.tv

The Inspiration

Gone in 60 Seconds was inspired by the 1974 film of the same name, directed by H.B. Halicki.

The Release Date

The film was released on June 9, 2000, in the United States.

The Plot

The movie follows a group of car thieves who are challenged to steal 50 high-end cars in one night to save their brother’s life.

The Title

The title “Gone in 60 Seconds” refers to the time it takes for the thieves to steal a car and make it disappear.

The Production Budget

The film had a production budget of approximately $90 million.

The Director

The movie was directed by Dominic Sena, known for his work on films like Swordfish and Kalifornia.

The Cast

The star-studded cast includes Nicolas Cage, Angelina Jolie, Giovanni Ribisi, Robert Duvall, and Delroy Lindo.

Nicolas Cage’s Character

Nicolas Cage plays the role of Randall “Memphis” Raines, a legendary car thief who comes out of retirement for one last job.

Angelina Jolie’s Character

Angelina Jolie portrays the character of Sara “Sway” Wayland, a skilled mechanic and love interest of Memphis.

The Car List

The film features an impressive lineup of cars, including a 1967 Shelby GT500, a 1962 Ferrari 250 GTO, and a 1999 Bentley Arnage.

The Car Chase Scenes

The movie is renowned for its thrilling car chase scenes, which were expertly choreographed and executed.

The Eleanor GT500

The 1967 Shelby GT500, nicknamed “Eleanor,” became an iconic car after the film’s release.

The film’s soundtrack features a mix of rock and electronic music, adding to the high-energy atmosphere.

The Box Office Success

“Gone in 60 Seconds” grossed over $237 million worldwide, making it a commercial success.

The Remake

The 2000 version of the film is a remake of the 1974 cult classic, with some changes made to modernize the story.

The Original Director

H.B. Halicki, the director of the original 1974 film, appears in the 2000 version as a pizza delivery guy.

The Filming Locations

The majority of the movie was filmed in Los Angeles, California, including iconic locations like Long Beach and San Pedro.

The Stunt Drivers

Professional stunt drivers were hired to perform the daring car maneuvers seen throughout the film.

The Chase Scene Record

The film holds the record for the most cars destroyed in a single movie chase scene, with over 150 vehicles totaled.

The Shelby Mustangs

Multiple Shelby Mustangs were used in the film, each customized to perform specific stunts.

The Car Auction

Several of the cars used in the movie were sold at auction after filming, fetching high prices due to their connection to the film.

The Production Team

The production team acquired a total of 400 cars for the film, including both props and vehicles actually used in the scenes.

The Special Effects

The movie utilized a combination of practical effects and CGI to enhance the action sequences.

The Original Gone in 60 Seconds Cars

Many of the cars featured in the film were borrowed from car enthusiasts and collectors who were fans of the original 1974 movie.

The Film’s Influences
