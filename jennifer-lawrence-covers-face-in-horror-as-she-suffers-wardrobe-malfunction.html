Jennifer Lawrence covers face in horror as she suffers wardrobe malfunction

Actress Jennifer Lawrence had quite the wardrobe malfunction as she gave a speech at an event held by Dior yesterday (November 20).

Jennifer Lawrence, who starred rom-com movie No Hard Feelings earlier this year, had a hilarious reaction to a wardrobe malfunction while speaking in front of a large group of people.

Jennifer Lawrence is ‘so sorry’ about wardrobe malfunction

The Hollywood star was in New York at the unveiling of the Saks Fifth Avenue and Dior ‘Carousel of Dreams’ 2023 holiday window displays. However, the belt on her waist had other plans and caused her to have a wardrobe malfunction when it popped open and fell to the floor.

Gasping, the 33-year-old exclaimed: “I’m so sorry, that was so loud, my belt popped off.”

To the event, the celebrity wore a long, luxurious-looking black coat over a white button-up shirt and black boots. Of course, her outfit contained the disastrous belt, which was wrapped around her waist.

She wore her hair down and accessorized with a black headband while donning a natural glam makeup and silver jewelry.

The ‘most annoying’ day of her life

In an interview with GQ, Timothée Chalamet revealed the first day he went onto set for their 2021 Netflix film Don’t Look Up, Jennifer was “annoyed”. The star admitted it was the “most annoying” day of her life after he was so excited to be leaving the house for the first time since being in quarantine due to the pandemic.

The Wonka star said: “By the time I got to set, I was buzz-ing. That was the day Jennifer said was the most annoying day of her life, working with me and Leo [DiCaprio].”

“Timothée was just excited to be out of the house, I think it was his first scene,” Jennifer later recalled. She went on to say that she was a “misery” on that particular day and that there was no bad blood. In fact, she called the two men “very nice.”

Jennifer’s future in The Hunger Games

The mom-of-one starred in four of the hit Dystopian films as Katniss Everdeen, and has recently told Variety that she would “totally” be up for reprising the role of The Girl on Fire.

“If Katniss ever could ever come back into my life, 100 percent,” she said.

However, a producer for the series has cast doubt. She said that it’s up to Suzanne Collins, who authored the books from which the films are adapted.

“If Suzanne has something to say, then she’ll write a book about it,” Nina said. However, she pointed out that she would be “thrilled” to work on something Katniss-centric again if given the opportunity.

“Honestly, as much as I love Katniss, I think her story is complete. And I think that Suzanne feels that her story is complete. But if that changes, and Suzanne has something she wants to say, and it involves Katniss, then I would be thrilled.

“But really any chance to be back in this world, and lead with Francis and Suzanne, I would take regardless of who it was about.”
