Five employees at Pontiac Postal Service die due to suspected gas leak

Five employees working at the same postal service center died within 14 moths of each other due to a suspected methane gas leak. 

The US postal service is being forced to resolve the gas buildup amid concerns for other employees' safety.  

The deaths have yet to be linked to the gas, but the United States Postal Service Inspector General's office recently issued a report detailing the investigation into the Michigan Metroplex Processing and Distribution Center on Glenwood Avenue in Pontiac, according to the Oakland Press.

Five employees working at the same postal service center have died within 14 moths of each other due to a suspected methane gas leak. According to an investigation, the methane detection system (pictured) at the Metroplex has not been functioning properly since March 2015, according to the report

The report reads: 'Based on our observations and review of the system log book, 3 the MDS at the Metroplex has not been functioning properly since March 2015. 

'Maintenance personnel repeatedly replaced the sensors and stated the continuous flashing amber warning light, which detects methane at 10 percent LEL, was due to sensor malfunction and not the buildup of methane gas.'

'However, if the system was operating as designed, the blue indicator light, which is designed to flash when there is a sensor malfunction, would have been illuminated during our observation.'

Methane is a colorless, odorless and potentially flammable gas created by decomposing bacteria which can be explosive.

The USPS inspector general's office also reported that they 'have not correlated these deaths to the safety concern at this time, but are continuing work in this area'. 

The deaths have all been attributed to natural causes.

Keesha Gray (pictured), 38, was one of the five employees who died at the facility. She was found dead in the parking lot of the facility last summer after working an overnight shift.

Keesha Gray, 38, was one of five employees who died at the facility. She was found dead in the parking lot of the facility last summer after working an overnight shift, according to the Oakland Press.

Authorities determined she died of bronchial asthma.

Mail Hauler's Union Local President Marlon Harris is concerned about his members' recent on-the-clock deaths.

He told Click on Detroit that he was told three deaths resulted from heart attacks. 

Harris said: 'We had one girl who walked out having an asthma attack, and she passed out in her car. 

'There's another guy who had a stroke.'

The Postal Service said the deaths were due to natural causes. 

They have had the methane levels in the plant tested and claims the levels are safe. 

Large concentrations of methane gas in enclosed areas can lead to suffocation. 

The facility opened in 2008 and it's the largest of its kind in the nation and employs about 1,200 postal workers. It's open 24 hours, seven days per week. 

As noted in the report, it was built on the site of the former vehicle manufacturing plant which included a foundry, a workshop or factory casting metal.

When the Postal Service built the facility, a gas venting system was installed under the foundation and inside the building.

A methane detection system (MDS) was also installed with sensors placed in various enclosed spaces, which is designed to detect and alert personnel of any accumulation of methane gas inside the facility. 

Postal service management will hire a contractor to inspect the system and fix any issues at the Pontiac location (pictured) by the end of May. They will also hire a firm to conduct periodic evaluations of the system to ensure methane levels are within guidelines for healthy measures

'The Postal Service did agree to evaluate and resolve any issues related to sensors on its methane detection system but again referenced the independent evaluation confirming there was no safety threat to employees.'
