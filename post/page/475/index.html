published on February 17, 2024
Just as “history” is not derived from his + story (or as some pun, her + story), as folk etymology would have it, neither is “woman” from womb/woe/wee + man. The early Old English (OE) wif – from the Proto-Germanic wibam, “woman” – originally denoted a female, and later became the Middle English (ME) wif, wiif, wyf.
