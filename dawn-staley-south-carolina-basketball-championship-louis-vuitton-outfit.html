Dawn Staley Rings In South Carolina's Win in a Full Louis Vuitton Outfit

On Sunday, coach Dawn Staley’s University of South Carolina Gamecocks won their third NCAA women’s basketball championship, defeating Iowa 87–75, despite the Hawkeyes’ rising star Caitlin Clark, to close out a perfect undefeated (38–0) season. As the game wrapped and the cheers from the audience rose to a roar, Staley grasped her well-deserved golden trophy while pink confetti rained down on her and her elated players.

As we witnessed the historic moment—and because this is Harper’s Bazaar, not ESPN—we couldn’t help but notice Staley’s outfit. The effortlessly cool coach and three-time gold Olympic medalist wore a silver Louis Vuitton jacket and white sneakers printed with the Vuitton monogram from the house’s recently released collaboration with rapper Tyler, the Creator.

Thien-An Truong/ISI Photos//Getty Images

The rodeo-inspired cropped calfskin leather jacket had black pockets on the sides and round silver studs at the neckline (her own nod to Cowboy Carter mania, perhaps?). Staley paired the jacket with a black top and sweats, and accessorized with a purple and green beaded necklace with her name on it, large silver hoop earrings, and of course, the standout sneakers.

Louis Vuitton x Tyler, the Creator LV Trainer

Louis Vuitton x Tyler, the Creator LV Trainer

Staley has always had a thing for great jackets and sneakers, but if ever there was a time for her to dress like the main character, this was her chance—and she took it.

Fashion and beauty have always been intertwined with women’s sports, but the past few years have brought a new era of style to the playing field. Courtside dressing and tunnel fits have proven to be a fresh form of individual self-expression in a space where sports where uniforms and a team mentality are required. And we’re not talking about the countless collaborations sports brands have done with pro athletes like Serena Williams and Simone Biles. We’re talking about track and field star Sha’Carri Richardson wearing bright crimson braids and long butterfly lashes while sprinting faster than any other woman in history; or soccer pro Trinity Rodman scoring championship-winning goals in a set of funky acrylic nails, then changing into a fur-lined denim Adidas suit after the game. We’re even talking about Louisiana State women’s basketball coach Kim Mulkey shouting plays from the sidelines in a bright pink feather-trimmed floral jacket.

Thien-An Truong/ISI Photos//Getty Images

South Carolina’s win is historic, and the beloved Staley deserved all the cheers—for both her inspiring leadership and her sharp style.
