A television news reporter for an Atlanta, Ga., station has been punched in the face by a man he was trying to interview while working on a story about a questionable business run by a felon.

Investigative reporter Jeff Chirico of CBS Atlanta was attempting to speak to the father of the business owner, following him and peppering him with questions for a story posted online on Jan. 30. The unresponsive interviewee was walking from his vehicle into EZ Auto Sales in Gainesville, Ga.

Donald Wilder just ignored Chirico as he entered the dealership, but Chirico held the door open and continued making inquiries, prompting Wilder to suddenly turn around and punch him in the face, knocking his microphone out of his hand and sending the reporter falling to the ground.

Wilder then emerges from the dealership and looms over the reporter, pointing down at him.

'Don't you dare attempt to go into my business. Don't you dare,' he scolds. 'Now you better get your sh** and getta gone before that man gets here.' It's not clear from the video which man he's referring to.

Chirico tells his attacker he will be calling the police and 'you will be arrested for assault.' Wilder responds that the news crew was just barging into his office uninvited.

A mean right: Wilder snaps, striking the reporter on the chin as he asks pointed questions about the business

'And you didn't tell us not to go in either,' Chirico replies, 'and you just punched me in the face.'

'Yeah, and I'm gonna punch you again,' Wilder fires back.

Following the attack, Donald Wilder is shown threatening the reporter once again while blood streams from his face as he makes a cell phone call.

'You're gonna be bleeding worse if you don't get off of my property,' he warns.

Floored: Chirico stumbles backward and falls to the ground after getting punched in the face

Lashing out: Wilder stands over Chirico after punching him and scolds him for trying to enter Wilder's used car dealership in Gainesville, Ga.

The alleged assailant's son is suspected of ripping people off in two Georgia counties through his business, which not only sells used cars but also prepares people's taxes.

CBS Atlanta reports that, every time 43-year-old Richard Wilder has been accused of wrongdoing, he closes up shop, moves to a different county and renames his business. The operation has undergone three name and location changes, most recently reinventing itself as EZ Auto Sales, in Gainesville, where Chirico caught up with Richard Wilder and his pugilistic pop.

Legal troubles: Richard Wilder relocated and reopened his business in violation of an order from Georgia's secretary of state

The younger Wilder allegedly opened EZ Auto Sales just weeks after he had been arrested by the Jackson County Sheriff's Office in September. Chirico and a CBS Atlanta crew recently paid a visit to the dealership to see whether Wilder was continuing the same sort of business practices that had gotten him charged with seven counts of theft by deception and one count of racketeering.

Police allege Wilder was depositing victims' tax refund checks into his account and then writing out checks to them for lesser amounts than the government had provided. One couple said he kept their tax refunds totaling $8,000.

A victim who had purchased a car from Wilder said the car salesman unilaterally cancelled the extended warranty on his Chevrolet Aveo because the repairs were getting too expensive.

Authorities say Wilder may have defrauded as many as 32 people.

Court records show Wilder was indicted in Gwinnett County for odometer fraud, forgery, computer theft and other violations in 2009 and 2010, according to CBS Atlanta. He reportedly pleaded guilty to two felony charges in 2011.

After Georgia's secretary of state issued a cease-and-desist order against him for running a used auto dealership without a license, he and family moved and re-branded their business.

Right before Chirico started questioning the Wilders, an undercover producer from the station found that the son was selling cars again, against the secretary of state's order. When Chirico arrived with a cameraman and started firing off questions in the used-car lot, the salesman claimed he was not Richard Wilder and fled inside the dealership.

Later the father pulled up and, after having questions lobbed at him while he attempted enter the business, he landed a right on Chirico's chin.

The reporter was not seriously injured.
