Beyonc Pairs Her Massive Wool Coat With Tall Boots and No Pants

Who needs pants when you have the biggest coat and the tallest boots? Certainly not Beyoncé. The music legend used those elements to serve up a more subtle country-western look as she continues to promote her top-charting eighth studio album, Cowboy Carter.

The “II Most Wanted” singer posted photos of her statement-making outfit on Instagram on Saturday. Her ensemble consisted of a black graphic T-shirt that honored country music trailblazer Linda Martell (who appears on Cowboy Carter tracks “Spaghettii” and “The Linda Martell Show”) with the cover art for Martell’s album Color Me Country, paired with black knit briefs with ribbing details by Chloé. She warmed up her outfit with a huge double-breasted wool maxi coat in a black, gray, orange, beige, and white checker pattern and slouchy thigh-high black leather boots with a tall heel and pointed-toe design, both from Chloé. Beyoncé accessorized with Chloé aviator sunglasses with brown-tinted lenses, the beige Fiamma crossbody bag from Ferragamo, several diamond statement rings, and layers of multicolored diamond- and crystal-adorned choker necklaces.

The Cécred founder wore her bronzy-blonde locks with white highlights down and curly with a middle part, and glammed up with matte, airbrushed makeup, complete with a pink-nude lip. Nodding to the American flag motif seen in Cowboy Carter promotional imagery, Beyoncé completed her look with red, white, and blue French-tip nails.

This is the latest in Beyoncé’s streak of cowgirl looks. She has brought the rodeo with her in a black leather Versace catsuit full of fringe and gold-stud details, a Dries Van Noten denim jumpsuit, a black Givenchy tuxedo with a coquette twist, a glamorous monochromatic brown outfit, and plenty of big-buckled belts, bolo ties, and cowboy hats.
