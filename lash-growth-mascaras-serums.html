The 9 Best Lash Growth Mascaras of 2024

There are very few beauty traits that are universally desired (after all, we all have our own preferences!), but we’re fairly confident that everyone wants longer and stronger lashes. Even if you don’t crave length and volume comparable to what you’d get from lash extensions, there’s just something about thicker and healthier lashes that make the face look youthful, vibrant, and, well, pretty.

If you’re not naturally blessed with a robust set of lashes, you may have already looked into methods for making them longer. Beyond temporary solutions like extensions and falsies, hair growth supplements and lash growth serums can go a long way towards increasing your lashes’ volume. But did you know that there are some mascara formulas that can actually promote lash growth? We consider these a two-for-one makeup moment: They enhance your beauty by building out your lashes instantly and over time, so long as you use them regularly.

Unfortunately, this is a very niche beauty category, with limited options available on the market — and if you’re looking for a budget-friendly pick, you’re up for an even bigger challenge. Still, we’ve found a top-notch selection (a drugstore buy included!) by scouring the web, putting the formulas to the test, and consulting with lash experts for their recommendations. In the end, we were thoroughly impressed with nine lash growth mascaras that truly make our lashes plush and full.

Best Overall

Kosas The Big Clean Volumizing + Lash Care Mascara

Credo Beauty

What We Love What We Don’t Love

Of all the formulas we tested, we felt this one yielded the most impressive results. That’s due to the fact that it delivers well-rounded benefits. Instead of just offering length or volume, The Big Clean provides both while curling and making lashes look denser. The combination of all of these benefits in one formula means that in just one coat, it appears like we’re wearing a set of ultra-plush, high-quality falsies. 

We can’t give credit solely to the formula — which contains lash-promoting castor oil, conditioning vitamin B, and thickening peptides — though. The bristled brush plays a big role. Its gently curved shape grabs every last lash and pulls them up for significant lift and a tighter curl, eliminating the need for an eyelash curler. The only aspect we don’t love about this formula is that it features a floral, almost rose-like fragrance that is a bit potent upon opening the tube, though we can’t smell it once it’s slicked on.

Benefits: Lengthens, volumizes, curls, and boosts density｜Key Ingredients: Castor seed oil, vitamin B5, biotinoyl tripeptide-1｜Waterproof: No

Best Drugstore

Rimmel Mascara

Amazon

What We Love What We Don’t Love

You may have noticed that this is the only formula below $20 on our list. Lash growth mascaras are hard to find — regardless of price — yet this budget-friendly pick turned out to be one of our favorites. The bristled brush features a slightly bowed-out shape that makes creating volume and length easy, whether you want to apply just one layer or multiple coats. 

Infused with lash-boosting castor seed oil, fortifying vitamin B5, and a proprietary complex known as Procapil (which uses biotinoyl tripeptide-1 and a selection of plant extracts to support lash growth with regular use), the affordable mascara strengthens lashes over time. However, if you’re someone who prefers to check out products in person before purchasing, note that this formula can be hard to find at a local drugstore, so it’ll be easier to just shop it online.

Benefits: Lengthens, volumizes｜Key Ingredients: Castor seed oil, vitamin B5, Procapil｜Waterproof: No

Best Strengthening

Nordstrom

What We Love What We Don’t Love

  • This formula doesn’t build easily, so if you like adding multiple coats to achieve a thicker look, you shouldn’t expect a very dramatic outcome.

This is one of the first lash-boosting mascaras we ever tried and remains one of our all-time favorites — and it’s one of professional makeup artist, Natalia Thomas’, favorites too. “It has a molded brush, so the formula doesn’t get too thick on the applicator,” she says. Best Volumizing

Grande Cosmetics Intense Thickening Mascara

Amazon

What We Love What We Don’t Love

Grande Cosmetics is known for its lash-lengthening and strengthening formulas, and while this product isn’t infused with the brand’s signature serum, it does contain castor seed oil, an ingredient recognized for its growth-promoting properties. 

We’re fans of the hourglass-like bristled brush — it’s one of our favorite styles for building volume, and it certainly does when paired with this mascara formula. After one or two coats, lashes look full and voluminous, (not dry or spidery) while being so lengthy that they occasionally get stuck on our eyebrows (!). The only persnickety complaint we have is about the tube, which is the widest we’ve ever seen for a mascara. Keep this in mind if you already struggle with a bulky makeup bag.

This mascara from Gwen Stefani’s makeup line has been receiving a great deal of hype — and it’s actually worthy. The precise tip of the brush is perfect for detail work, like coating tiny inner and bottom lashes, but be sure to give it a quick wipe with a paper towel first — the unique spherical shape can hold a lot of product, and you don’t want to overload your lashes with it.

Best Lengthening

What We Love What We Don’t Love

If you’ve never tried a tubing formula before, you’re in for a treat — this award-winning product comes in three shades (including a unique gray-blue) and has tens of thousands of positive reviews. If your lashes grow downwards, you may not have the best experience with this, though. The formula is a bit heavier than other mascaras we’re used to which makes it hard for our lashes to hold a curl, but they do stay soft!

What to Keep in Mind

Wand 

If you’re already a mascara user, you know that there are really only two options out there when it comes to wands: Brushes with molded or regular bristles. The molded ones are often made of materials like plastic or silicone. Depending on the custom shape, they may look like a fairly standard brush, like Thrive Causemetics Liquid Lash Extensions Mascara, or a medieval device designed to force your lashes into submission thanks to little add-ons, like the spherical spiked tip at the end of the wand inside Gxve by Gwen Stefani Can’t Stop Staring Clean Lengthening & Lifting Mascara. Traditional bristles are the type of wands that are most recognizable and are typically made using twisted wire to create a brush effect (like the one that comes with Grande Cosmetics GrandeDRAMA Intense Thickening Mascara).

Truthfully, there’s no “better” wand between the two options. “I really find that that’s a matter of preference,” says Thomas. “It’s up to the user whether they prefer a brush that carries more product or helps them to define and separate better.” Traditional wands tend to hold a lot more mascara than molded brushes as there are more nooks and crannies between wire bristles for the formula to get into — this means you’ll be able to load on some significant pigment for a more bolder look with boosted volume. Molded brushes, especially those that have spiky tips, are helpful for creating separation and definition, says Thomas.

Formula

When seeking out a lash growth mascara, the formula is everything — it needs to be infused with active ingredients that were selected specifically for their ability to promote longer, thicker lashes, while helping to strengthen and condition the lashes you already have. “Choose products that contain lash-nourishing ingredients, like keratin, panthenol (vitamin B5), and plant extracts such as red clover extract, and amino acids,” recommends Vincent. These ingredients will help strengthen your lashes, making it easier for them to grow more healthily and thus, longer. For our list, we selected mascaras that exclusively have ingredients like these in their formulas.

One of our favorite plant extracts to look for in lash growth formulas is castor seed oil. Many people use this natural ingredient as a homeopathic remedy, applying it to their lashes, brows, and scalps to help stimulate hair growth. There’s something to it, though — ricinoleic acid is one of the most concentrated fatty acids found in castor seed oil, and it’s been proven to have an effect on hair growth. Remember, the key to seeing success from lash growth formulas is using them every day. That’s why we like the idea of mascara infused with ingredients that support lash growth — it’s easy for us to remember to use our mascara!

If you are someone that prefers a waterproof formula, we recommend only using it sporadically and applying one that has lash growth properties like Urban Decay Perversion Waterproof Mascara. “Most waterproof formulas have formaldehyde, and even if they do not contain this ingredient, they are especially difficult to remove — the friction of removal causes your lashes to fall out more readily,” warns Thomas. 

Your Questions, Answered

What helps your eyelashes grow?

Treating your eyelashes gently and with care will go a long way towards supporting healthy growth. Often, it’s certain behaviors that are inhibiting your lashes from getting lengthy. “External factors include damage from rubbing too harshly when removing eye makeup, reaction to mascaras or eyeliners, or irritation from lash extensions,” says Dani Kimiko Vincent, professional makeup artist and founder of Kimiko. 

Your makeup technique can also play a role. For example, you should never curl lashes once you’ve already applied mascara. “This can tear out lashes when the curler is opened and is damaging, so ensure you’re only curling bare lashes,” Vincent adds. 

Lastly, consider how you sleep. Of course, you’ll be going to bed after fully removing your makeup (right?), but sleeping on your face can wreak havoc on your lashes. “If you are a face sleeper, a silk pillowcase can be especially helpful for retaining moisture in your skin and hair,” recommends Thomas.

Beyond using mascaras with nourishing ingredients and avoiding bad behaviors, you can supplement your diet with nutrients that facilitate hair growth. Some to look for are biotin (found in eggs), fatty acids (in oily fish, like salmon), and vitamin B (in nuts and seeds). You can also go the supplement route. Brittany Lo, professional makeup artist and founder of Beia, recommends seeking out prenatal vitamins, while Vincent likes Nutrafol. Just be sure to ask your doctor before incorporating anything new into your diet, supplements included.

Of course, incorporating lash serums or mascaras with strengthening and conditioning ingredients can also make a difference. Any of the mascaras on our list will enhance lashes with continued use, but Kosas The Big Clean Mascara really stands out for its mix of lash-promoting castor oil, conditioning vitamin B, and thickening peptides.

Can mascara affect lash growth?

Not directly, per se. “Mascara in and of itself does not affect lash growth, but not fully removing it or being harsh when removing can,” says Vincent, who (like Thomas) advises against regular use of waterproof mascara as rubbing your lashes to cleanse them can adversely affect lash fullness. 

“If you struggle with your mascara smudging and you lean towards using a waterproof mascara, try switching to a tubing mascara, which can be simply removed with warm water,” suggests Vincent. Thrive Causemetics Liquid Lash Extensions Mascara is a solid example of this — regular use of a tubing mascara will help them recover from waterproof mascara. When you use traditional mascara formulas, including waterproof, Lo recommends using a bi-phase makeup remover, which contains oil to help break down durable formulas. 

It’s also important to keep your lash routine hygienic. Eye infections can inhibit lash growth, and a beauty routine can get pretty filthy if you’re not paying attention. “Make sure you are replacing your mascara every couple of months to ensure you're not using a tube infected with bacteria,” warns Lo. We recommend labeling your mascara with the date you started using it — this way, you can keep track of how long it’s been on rotation and when you should ditch it for a new, clean tube.

What is the healthiest mascara for your eyelashes?

While we fully endorse the mascaras on this list, which are formulated with ingredients to support healthy and happy lashes (so long as you’re taking them off every night!), taking a break from mascara will really help your lashes get stronger. “Try opting for a lash lift and tint which curls your lashes at the root and tints them darker so that you can skip the mascara altogether,” suggests Thomas. 

You can also just give them a chance to breathe. “When your lashes are feeling weak, I suggest using a darker liner, curling the lashes, and foregoing mascara unless it's necessary,” Lo says. When you’re feeling ready for mascara, select one of the above formulas — they’ll help fortify your lashes over time for a lush and healthy look.
