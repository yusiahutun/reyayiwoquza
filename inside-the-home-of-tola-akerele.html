Inside the home of... Tola Akerele

We earn a commission for products purchased through some links in this article.

1

Sitting area

Courtesy

"This chair was created in our iDesign workshop. My inspiration when designing it was the traditional Yoruba beaded chair used in the king’s palaces from the South West of Nigeria. My interpretation is more fluid and the form is softer than the traditional version. The wood frame is also a departure from the original. The seating area is made of tiny hand sewn white beads; it's fit for a queen."

2

The living room

Courtesy

"This is where we gather as a family to read with our girls. The artwork on the left was one of the first pieces I ever bought over 25 years ago; it's by a terrific artist called Chief Muraina Oyelami. It just draws you in."

3

The living room

Courtesy

"The house had not been lived in for years when we moved in, but the staircase was such a great feature. Everything else was such a wreck, but the staircase stood firm. I love the way it floats. We also kept the original Terrazzo flooring which is also very typical for houses built in the '70s - it's great as it keeps the house cool."

4

The dining area

Courtesy

"This is definitely the heart of our home. We spend hours here with family and friends sampling my different recipes and partially responsible for me deciding to create the Orishirishi cookbook. It’s also great for entertaining as it opens up to the veranda and garden. The lights are by a Portuguese company called Mambo. This piece of art always sparks up a good conversation - there’s so much in it."

5

The living space

Courtesy

"This was acquired from my frequent rummaging in our main local arts and crafts market. It’s a West African stool, but what makes it original are the bold colours and symbolism. It's quite low, so was a great piece for the girls when they were younger."

6

The study

Courtesy

"An invaluable space since Covid, this were all the work gets done. It's peaceful and soothing. The chairs were created in the iDesign workshop and were inspired by mid-century Danish furniture - I just love the curves. The image on the wall reminds me of my girls, it’s by a young artist called Adulphina."

7

The bathroom

Courtesy

"This my favourite bathroom. We opted for shells rather than tiles, which felt a little experimental. Living close to the beach, we gathered shells over time and worked with an artist to create image and motifs on the walls. It’s a great space for escapism."

8

The living room

Courtesy

"I love Moroccan design and the intricate brass motifs used for lighting. This piece was purchased from Yahya while in Morocco on a sourcing trip. I just love it."

9

The veranda

Courtesy

"This chair was from a series of outdoor bamboo furniture designed at iDesign for us a few years ago. The area overlooks the garden and it's where I love to start my day, it’s very serene. The planters are from Bamidele Designs a local supplier."

The bedroom
