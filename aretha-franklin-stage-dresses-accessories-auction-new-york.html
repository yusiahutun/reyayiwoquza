Aretha Franklin's most famous outfits and accessories to go up for auction

Aretha Franklin's most famous outfits and accessories are to be sold at auction in New York on November 10.

More than 30 of her history-making looks will go on display between November 5 to 9 with highlights including the red sequinned dress she donned on stage at Radio City Hall back in 1991.

Related Story

The exhibition will also feature a pastel-hued coat the late singer wore on a television programme marking Oprah Winfrey's 40th birthday.

While the St. John gold and white embroidered number she wore to the 1999 National Medal of Arts Ceremony will also prove a hit with visitors.

Wally McNamee//Getty Images

The St. John knit number she donned on stage at Bill Clinton’s nomination gala in 1997 will go up for auction

The 'Queen of Soul' passed away last month aged 76 and her Detroit-based funeral saw the likes of Stevie Wonder, Barack Obama and Ariana Grande pay their respects.

Related Story

Martin Nolan, executive director of Julien’s Auctions, said:"On and off the stage, Ms Franklin was also a great fashion legend."

"This collection of her most show-stopping looks demonstrates why she was not only the Queen of Soul but the Queen of Style."
