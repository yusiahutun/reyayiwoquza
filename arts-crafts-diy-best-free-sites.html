The 6 Best Free Sites for DIY Arts and Crafts

Sometimes, it's nice to take a little time away from our devices and do something different. But it's not always easy to find an activity interesting enough to keep us occupied for long enough.

Why not give your creative side a chance? Here, we look at six free websites that offer fun and affordable DIY arts and crafts ideas to keep your mind as well as your hands busy.

1. Pinterest

Pinterest is a very popular site, as you probably already know, with its massive stock of creative ideas and inspiration. Naturally, there's no shortage of ideas for DIY arts and crafts either. With Pinterest, you can make your searches very specific, and you'll be met with an endless list of inspirations.

What's more, when you create a Pinterest account, you can save the posts, or pins, that you'd like to revisit later. You can also follow pages you like or even post your own content if you're feeling inspired. Download your pinned photos to store on your device or print them out as a guide for your arts and crafts project.

2. Happiness Is Homemade

Happiness Is Homemade is a great resource that offers arts and crafts ideas to people of all ages. We love that it includes seasonal ideas such as that for Thanksgiving, Christmas, or Halloween, and there are also a variety of projects you can occupy yourself with year-round. Simply click on Crafts + DIY to find the perfect project for you.

This site doesn't just offer ideas, but also printable pieces, such as coloring templates, stencils, and foldable 3D grids, which you can find in the Printables section. What's more, this section offers free printable games, like crosswords and bingo sheets, as well as free home decor graphics.

There's also a wide range of SVG (Scalable Vector Graphics) files on offer for those who want to grab some pre-designed stickers and labels for free.

3. Arty Crafty Kids

As the site name suggests, this one is great for the little ones. If you have kids, you'll know how difficult it can be to entertain them during evenings, weekends, and holidays, especially when it's too cold or wet to do something outside. So, if you want to keep them busy, let them get creative!

Arty Crafty Kids is filled with an array of different ideas for children, regardless of their interests. Print free coloring sheets, dot-to-dot sheets, or try out some adorable DIY bookmark ideas.

You can also help your little ones learn how to draw with the site's drawing tutorials and tips. There's even a section that offers lessons in letters and numbers, as well as puzzles to get their minds working.

The site offers a membership for those who want full access to all the templates, tutorials, and learning resources available. This is a great way to keep your kids occupied while letting them learn new things.

4. Good Housekeeping

You've probably heard of Good Housekeeping; it's a popular paper publication, but it also has its own website, where you can find some amazing DIY and creative ideas. Open the menu and navigate to Home Ideas > Crafts & DIY Projects, and you'll have access to an array of arts and crafts inspiration for children and adults.

In this section, you can learn how to stitch, get DIY crafts ideas for kids, and find gorgeous DIY homeware tutorials. On top of this, you can learn how to make sustainable kitchenware, such as beeswax wraps, and how to upcycle your old furniture or ornaments into gorgeous new pieces. You can even learn how to knit your own clothing!

Good Housekeeping also offers decoration ideas, so you can plan how you're going to display your arts and crafts projects in your home. What's not to love about your very own DIY decor?

5. Craftsy Hacks

Craftsy Hacks is an amazing resource for cute, minimalist, and affordable arts and crafts ideas. On this site, you can learn how to make paper lanterns, clay bowls, plant pots, or even dreamcatchers, all from scratch. If you're feeling fashionable, you can learn how to make your own crystal necklaces, wire rings, and macrame earrings.

There are also sections for planning, organization, and cleaning, so you can get your home in tip-top shape while creating some gorgeous pieces to put on display. These sections include DIY cleaning solutions, cleaning hacks, and organizational inspiration. It's really an all-in-one website when it comes to caring for your home!

If you're looking to give your friends and family a little love too, there's a great section for gift ideas. Be it homemade or store-bought, you'll never find yourself giving them a bland pair of socks or soap set again.

6. Sarah Maker

The Sarah Maker crafts website, founded by a DIY enthusiast, is a great resource for finding arts and crafts ideas as well as learning new skills. Here, you can learn how to make your own quilts, clay jewelry, tie-dye clothing, festive ornaments, friendship bracelets, face masks, and embroider gorgeous patterns and badges.

There's a designated section for crochet, where you can learn how to crochet scarves, hats, blankets, and more. The step-by-step crochet tutorials for beginners are great for those who are new to this hobby.

Get Inspired for Your Next Arts and Crafts Project

It can be hard to come up with your own ideas for DIY projects, especially if you're not a seasoned arts and crafts enthusiast. But with the sites we've listed here, you'll have access to seemingly endless fun and creative DIY ideas, and for free too. Happy crafting!
