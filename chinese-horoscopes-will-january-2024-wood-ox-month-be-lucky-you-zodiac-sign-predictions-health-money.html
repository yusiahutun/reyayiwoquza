Chinese horoscopes: will January 2024 Wood Ox month be lucky for you? Zodiac sign predictions for he

Wood Ox Month: January 6 to February 3

Elements of the month: Yin Water / Wood

Energy of the Ox: Hardworking, principled, leadership, intuitive

The Ox corresponds to the hours between: 1am-3am

The twelfth and last month of the Chinese calendar year is represented by the Ox, the final month of winter in the Northern Hemisphere.

Those born in the darkest hours reach out to the light. They dislike having miserable people around them and try to bring laughter to others. They may be jokers, and this sometimes works against them. At critical moments, they may not be taken seriously.

This Ox month is all about business, making money and moving forward, while listening more and talking less. This month brings balance and calm as we brace ourselves for the transition period between the two new years. It’s time for strategy and planning as we prepare for the Lunar New Year – my all-time favourite time of the year.

Be bold, be bullish!

Mythologically, the Ox was the messenger between Heaven and Earth, bringing dreams – strange images that prepare us for the year ahead while helping to process the one that has passed. The ancient Chinese pictogram of the Ox (chou) shows a hand, which symbolises the means by which the invisible becomes visible through action and expression. Life is cyclical, offering us the opportunity to make corrections and achieve our goals with another year of wisdom to draw on. This is the time of the year for rebirth, renewal and reflection.

Those born in the Year of the Ox (1949, 1961, 1973, 1985, 1997, 2009) are realistic and fair-minded. They are known to be sensitive, intuitive, insightful – bringing the unconscious into consciousness. They are also steady, methodical and reliable, so not usually fast movers. Once they make up their minds, they would get things finished in their own pace.

For those born during the Ox Month of January, their best suited careers are ones that require specific expertise or continual gaining of knowledge. These include lawyer, doctor, teacher, author, social worker, writer, editor, government officer, police officer, technician, politician, office clerk or consultant.

Those born on an Ox Day are talented, smart, and motivated. Your early years may have been complicated, but there may be great possibility to achieve potential success in middle age. You may enjoy an easier old age when all your needs and comforts will be met.

If you have one or more Ox signs in your birth chart, your Ox traits will be magnified during January, your specific astrological month.

Famous Oxen include Carl Jung, Sigmund Freud, Barack Obama, Peter Jackson, Princess Diana, Jeremy Corbyn and Annie Leibovitz.
