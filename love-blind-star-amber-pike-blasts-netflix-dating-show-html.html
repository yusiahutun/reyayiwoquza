Love is Blind star Amber Pike BLASTS the Netflix dating show

Just a week after the controversial 'live' Season 4 finale that was delayed 75 minutes, one of the first Love Is Blind winners is calling out the show.

Amber Pike, 31, who married Matt Barnett at the end of the show's first season in 2018 and is still together to this day, didn't pull any punches on Instagram this month.

The former reality star and her husband shared new snaps from their Easter celebration, where a fan asked who their 'favorite couple' was from Season 4.

Pike responded to the comment, stating, 'we don’t watch LIB,' which surprised many of her 1.3 million followers.

The commenter who asked who her favorite couple was said she was surprised because the show is where she found, 'the love of your life,' but Pike said, 'We are together IN SPITE of that show not because of it.'

Another fan asked if they 'argued' with the show's creators, since they didn't appear on the Season 1 reunion show, Pike said, 'I told them to stay away from us.'

Another fan - @ne_jr_12 - called out Pike, who was listed as an 'Ex-Tank Mechanic' on the show, for blasting the show.

'But you were able to meet Burnett on the show, who saved you from being homeless and having student loans... and you obviously seem to have a better life now, but isn't that because of the show? I don't know what went on behind the scenes, and I know that being on a reality show can be stressful, but you signed up for it by choice. It is too convenient for you to suddenly distance yourself from the show now. LIB, the media, and the audience behind the popularity of the show made you what you are today,' the fan said, though Pike clearly disagreed with the fan's assessment.

'Except that literally NONE of what you said was true,' Pike commented.

The news comes after other former Love Is Blind contestants alleging that the show's producers deprived them of food and water during the pods.

Season 2 stars Danielle Ruhl and Nick Thompson revealed to Insider that contestants were deprived of food, water, sleep and 'mental health assistance.'

Cast members claimed to have suffered, 'emotional breakdowns' as a result of the show's producers neglecting the contestants.

The contestants alleged they were forced to film up to 20 hours a day, and while they were frequently provided alcohol, they weren't given much time to sleep and rarely saw sunlight.

Ruhl claimed that she fainted during the pod period - where contestants were only in specific rooms - because she hadn't eaten, slept and had minimal amounts of water.

When a crew member insisted she take a COVID-19 test, after the tested negative she was rushed right back in to film a confessional. 

Ruhl alleged that producers also ignored her pleas for help with her mental health after suffering a panic attack in Mexico.

'I kept telling them, "I don't trust myself. I've tried committing suicide before. I'm having suicidal thoughts. I don't think I can continue in this,"' she recalled.

The show's producers, Kinetic Content, released a statement denying the allegations in the report.

'The wellbeing of our participants is of paramount importance to Kinetic. We have rigorous protocols in place to care for each person before, during, and after filming,' the statement read. 

Another Season 1 contestant, Danielle Drouin, backed up the allegations, particularly in regards to the sleep deprivation.  

'The sleep deprivation was real. I feel like they do it on purpose because they're trying to break you. They want you on your edge,' she said
